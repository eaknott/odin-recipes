Chicken Tikka Masala

Description

Spice up your life! If you're like me, you're a big fan of delicious Indian food and you have a well-stocked spice cabinet. I've had the pleasure of cooking a few of my favorite Indian dishes, and I've found that this one by far is the tastiest and easiest to make. Best of both worlds! It can be enjoyed with Basmati rice, or any rice of your choice, or even with some simply sauteed vegetables.

The best part of this beautiful dish is the aromatic and healthy spices (did I mention the spices?). Some additional prep time is needed to chop and marinate the chicken. In general, I like to marinate the chicken in the fridge for 30-60 minutes. However, you can marinate it for as little time as 10 minutes, or for as long as 8-10 hours (overnight). The longer the better, but if you're pressed on time, don't worry. This meal will still be full of flavor!

Let's get started!

Ingredients

For the chicken marinade:

  • 28 ounces (800 g) of boneless and skinless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup of plain yogurt (or Greek yogurt)
  • 1 1/2 tbsp of fresh garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp of fresh ginger, minced (or 1 tsp of ginger powder)
  • 2 tsp of garam masala
  • 1 tsp of turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp of cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp of red chili powder
  • 1 tsp of salt

For the sauce:

  • 3 tbsp of vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp of butter
  • 1/2 a yellow onion or 1 small white onion, diced
  • 1 1/2 tbsp of garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp of ginger, minced (or 1 tsp of ginger powder)
  • 1 1/2 tsp of garam masala
  • 1 1/2 tsp of cumin powder
  • 1 tsp of turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp of ground coriander
  • 14 ounces (400 g) of tomato puree
  • 1 tsp of red chili powder
  • 1 tsp of salt
  • 1 1/4 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 tsp of brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup of water if needed
  • Optional: 4 tbsp of fresh cilantro to garnish
