Vegetarian Shepherd's Pie

Description

This classic English dish is one of my favorites. Traditionally made with ground lamb or ground beef, this recipe puts a spin on a cottage pie that can be enjoyed by vegetarians and meat lovers alike! It's great for any time of the year, but especially welcome in the cooler seasons.

Something new that I got to try with this recipe is adding the Greek yogurt to the mashed potatoes. I have to say I was skeptical at first, since I normally enjoy the mashed potatoes with butter, but the Greek yogurt certainly changed my mind! A couple of heaping dollops added to your mashed potatoes gives it a tangy flavor that really complements the richness of the red wine gravy.

This fantastic recipe is so easy, anyone could make it. A personal tip that I like to do for this dish is chop the veggies fairly small (smaller than bite-size, about one centimeter wide). This may take a bit more prep work and chopping, but the smaller pieces not only cook faster but also give a texture that is much like the ground meat that is traditionally used! Of course, this is entirely up to you.

Let's get cooking!

Ingredients

Vegetarian Shepherd's Pie Filling:

  • 3 tbsp of olive oil or avocado oil
  • 3 shallots, minced (or substitute with onion, minced)
  • 16 ounces of white mushrooms, sliced
  • 16-20 ounces of green beans, chopped
  • 6 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • fresh or dried herbs to taste (I like to use Rosemary, Thyme, and a Bay leaf)
  • 2 tbsp of tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of red wine
  • 1-2 cups of veggie broth
  • salt and pepper to taste

Mashed Potatoes:

  • 6-8 Yukon Gold potatoes (about 2 pounds)
  • 1/2 cup of Greek Yogurt
  • 1/4 cup of butter or olive oil
  • salt and pepper to taste
